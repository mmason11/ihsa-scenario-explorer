Scenario overrides

Adjust a school's enrollment, toggle the 1.65× multiplier or success-factor class bump per sport, or move it to a different sectional — the Scenario Explorer and Full Data tabs reflect it immediately. Reclassification uses IHSA's published enrollment cutoffs (current one-year cycle, ihsa.org/Schools/Enrollments-Classifications) for baseball, softball, boys/girls basketball, girls volleyball, and boys/girls soccer — the only sports this tool classifies by enrollment. These are two separate, independent mechanisms: the 1.65× multiplier applies to every private-school program by default unless waived — for boys soccer/girls volleyball/boys & girls basketball the checkbox default comes from IHSA's own published waiver flag (see the IHSA-flag column on the Schools tab); other sports fall back to assuming every private program is multiplied. Public schools aren't eligible and don't get the checkbox at all. The success factor is also private-only: a direct one-class bump (e.g. 2A→3A) for a private program that reached the state final 4 twice in the rolling three seasons before the one in question, independent of enrollment — same sports get IHSA's own published flag as the default (checkbox marked "(auto)"); others fall back to reconstructing it from real state-tournament results back to 2022-23. Both are still editable per school if you want to explore a different assumption. Neither mechanism accounts for co-op programs, which classify on their combined enrollment rather than any one member's own figure — also flagged directly on the Schools tab where published. A Proposed-system move is only offered while the school is in the sport/class field currently selected above (that's what determines the Public/Private path names available). Overrides live only in this browser (not saved/shared). Classification cutoffs and the multiplier value itself are edited from the Schools tab, since those apply sport-wide (or tool-wide) rather than to one school.

Classification cutoffs

Drag or type a class's max-enrollment boundary to reclassify every school in the sport selected above at once — e.g. raise 1A's ceiling and see who moves into it. The top class is always "and over" and isn't editable.

Private-school multiplier

The official multiplier is 1.65×, applied by default to every private program (waivable per school/sport in Scenario Overrides). Changing it here models a different policy value across every sport and school at once — e.g. what would 1.5× or 2× do to classifications overall.

Class summary —

School list

Method: where actual IHSA assignments are unavailable, schools are grouped by recursive geographic bisection (balanced, contiguous splits), mirroring how sectional and regional boundaries are drawn; regional subdivisions are always modeled at the chosen size. Travel is one-way straight-line distance to the host site: the named IHSA sectional host where assignments are actual, otherwise the most centrally located member school (host schools travel 0). Any sectional's host can be reassigned from its roster panel — to another member, any IHSA school, or a custom venue by address (geocoded via OpenStreetMap). School locations are city-level ZIP centroids, so figures understate true road miles by roughly 15–25% and Chicago-to-Chicago trips read as zero. Recommended private paths = 8 × private share of the field, rounded, kept between 1 and 4. Sources: 2025 girls flag football pairings; 2025–26 boys/girls lacrosse and boys volleyball assignments (Elgin and O'Fallon volleyball sectionals include sub-sectionals); 2024–25 water polo pairings. Charter and other non-boundaried public schools are treated as public. Verify status flags marked for review before publication. Enrollment figures (Scenario Overrides tab) are each school's base ISBE-derived enrollment prior to any 1.65× success multiplier.
